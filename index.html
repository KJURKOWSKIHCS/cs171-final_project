Etty Hillesum, Victim

  • January 1914: Born in small town on Middelburg
  • 1924: Hillesum grew up in Deventer, a small city in the east of the Netherlands.
  • 1932: Took her first degree in law in Amsterdam and then enrolled in the faculty of Slavonic Languages to study Russian
  • March 1941: Starts her diary when the Germans occupy the Netherlands for nearly one year.
  • July 1942: Took on administrative duties for the Jewish Council
  • 1942: Personal status was suddenly revoked and she became a camp internee along with her father, mother and brother Mischa
  • September 1943: Family were deported from Westerbork to Auschwitz
  • November 1943: Etty died in Auschwitz

Joseph Mandrowitz, Survivor

  • 1925: Born in Czermierniki, Poland on a ranch
  • May 1943: Ranch was taken over by SS and Joseph ended up working for them
  • May 1943: Joseph was sent to Majdanek Prisoner Camp
  • June 1943: Sent to Auschwitz
  • 1943: Ends up in a hospital where he was a victim of the experiments of Joseph Mengele
  • 1944: Joseph was sent on a death march to Sachenhausen-Oranienburg
  • December 1944: Arrives at Buchenwald Labor Camp
  • 1945: Joseph was transported to Theresienstadt by a cargo train
  • May 1945: Joseph is liberated by the Soviets
  • Post war: Moves to U.S. where he stayed until his death on May 9, 2016

Karl Gorath, Survivor

  • Born in Bad Zwishenahn in northern Germany
  • Family moves to port of Bremerhaven
  • Karl was arrested and send to concentration camp in Neuengamme for homosexuality
  • Transferred to work at the prisoner hospital at the Wittenberg subcamp
  • Refused to decrease rations for Polish prisoners. As punishment, he was sent to Auschwitz
  • Karl was liberated in Auschwitz in 1945
